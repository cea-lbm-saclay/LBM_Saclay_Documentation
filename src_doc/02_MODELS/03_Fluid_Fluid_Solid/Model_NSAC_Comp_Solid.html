Model of Navier-Stokes/Allen-Cahn/Composition interacting with a solid phase

The previous Model of Navier-Stokes/Conservative Allen-Cahn (CAC)/Composition is extended here to interact with a solid phase which is described by a new function noted \(\psi\). In the input file, the problem name NSAC_Comp remains unchanged. The new function is taken into account through new sections and options in the input file.

Mathematical model

As already said, in this model we introduce of a new field \(\psi\equiv\psi(\boldsymbol{x},t)\) for solid phase. \(\phi\) is the phase-field of the first fluid phase, whereas \(\varphi\) is the phase-field of the second fluid phase. In that model \(\psi\) is defined by its initial condition and evolves with a PDE, \(\phi\) is defined by its initial condition and evolves with a PDE and \(\varphi\) is obtained by the closure relationship:

(118)\[\varphi(\boldsymbol{x},t)=1-\phi(\boldsymbol{x},t)-\psi(\boldsymbol{x},t)\]

Mathematical model

Mass balance equation

The mass balance equation writes:

(119)\[\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot\boldsymbol{v}=-\boldsymbol{u}_{s}\cdot\boldsymbol{\nabla}\psi\]

where the fluid velocity is noted \(\boldsymbol{u}\) and a variable change is introduced \(\boldsymbol{v}=(1-\psi)\boldsymbol{u}\). In the solid phase \(\psi=1\) and \(\boldsymbol{v}=\boldsymbol{0}\). In the right-hand side, \(\boldsymbol{u}_{s}\) is the velocity of the solid phase which must be known and imposed as input parameter.

Impulsion balance equation

The impulsion balance equation writes:

(120)\[\varrho\left[\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{v}}{\partial t}+\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot(\boldsymbol{v}\boldsymbol{u})\right]=-(1-\psi)\boldsymbol{\nabla}p_{h}+\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot\left[\eta\left(\boldsymbol{\nabla}\boldsymbol{v}+(\boldsymbol{\nabla}\boldsymbol{v})^{T}\right)\right]+(1-\psi)\boldsymbol{F}_{tot}\]

If \(\psi=1\), all terms of this equation cancel.

Phase-field equation

The phase-field equation is modified to take into account the both fields \(\psi\) and \(\varphi\):

(121)\[\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}+\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot(\boldsymbol{u}\phi)=\boldsymbol{\nabla}\cdot\left\{ M_{\phi}\left[\boldsymbol{\nabla}\phi-\frac{4}{W_{\phi}}\phi(1-\phi)\boldsymbol{n}_{\phi}+\boldsymbol{\mathscr{L}}(\phi,\varphi,\psi)\right]\right\}\]

In right-hand side of Eq. (121), a Lagrange multiplier \(\boldsymbol{\mathscr{L}}\) is introduced to impose the constrainst (118). Its expression is:

(122)\[\boldsymbol{\mathscr{L}}(\phi,\varphi,\psi)=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{4}{W_{\psi}}\psi(1-\psi)\boldsymbol{n}_{\psi}+\frac{4}{W_{\phi}}\phi(1-\phi)\boldsymbol{n}_{\phi}+\frac{4}{W_{\varphi}}\varphi(1-\varphi)\boldsymbol{n}_{\varphi}\right]\]

For every phase-field, the unit normal vector of the interface is defined by

(123)\[\boldsymbol{n}_{f}=\frac{\boldsymbol{\nabla}f}{\bigl|\boldsymbol{\nabla}f\bigr|}\]

where \(f=\psi,\phi,\varphi\).

Solid phase transport equation

Finally the evolution equation on \(\psi\) is a standard advection equation:

(124)\[\frac{\partial\psi}{\partial t}+\boldsymbol{u}_{s}\cdot\boldsymbol{\nabla}\psi=0\]

Let us mention that the composition equation remains unchanged in that version of model. Integration of \(\psi\): in that equation could be done in future work.

Force terms

Force terms

Total force

The total force term \(\boldsymbol{F}_{tot}\) is the sum of two forces: the capillary force \(\boldsymbol{F}_{c}\) and the gravity force \(\boldsymbol{F}_{g}\).

(125)\[\boldsymbol{F}_{tot} = \boldsymbol{F}_{c} + \boldsymbol{F}_{g}\]
Gravity force

The gravity force is defined by

(126)\[\boldsymbol{F}_{g}=\varrho(\boldsymbol{\phi})\boldsymbol{g}\]

where \(\boldsymbol{\phi}=(\phi_{0},\phi_{1},\phi_{2})\) and we have introduced the notations \(\phi_{0}\equiv\varphi\), \(\phi_{1}\equiv\phi\) and \(\phi_{2}\equiv\psi\).

Capillary force
(127)\[\boldsymbol{F}_{c}=\mu_{\phi}\boldsymbol{\nabla}\phi+\mu_{\varphi}\boldsymbol{\nabla}\varphi+\mu_{\psi}\boldsymbol{\nabla}\psi\]

The first term of the righ-hand side corresponds to the capillary force between fluid 1 (\(\phi\)) and fluid 2 (\(\varphi\)) which involves the surface tension \(\sigma_{12}\). The second term corresponds to the capillary force between fluid 1 (\(\phi\)) and solid (\(\psi\)) which involves the surface tension \(\sigma_{1s}\). Finally, the last term corresponds to the capillary force between fluid 2 (\(\phi\)) and solid (\(\psi\)) which involves the surface tension \(\sigma_{2s}\).

Chemical potentials

With the notations of gravity force, the chemical potentials write

(128)\[\mu_{\phi_{k}}(\boldsymbol{x},t)=\frac{4\gamma_{T}}{W}\sum_{\ell\neq k}\left[\frac{1}{\gamma_{\ell}}\left(\frac{\partial f_{dw}}{\partial\phi_{k}}-\frac{\partial f_{dw}}{\partial\phi_{\ell}}\right)\right]-\frac{3}{4}W\gamma_{k}\boldsymbol{\nabla}^{2}\phi_{k}\]

Closure relationships

(129)\[\varrho=\phi\rho_1+\varphi\rho_2+\psi\rho_{s}\]

where \(\rho_1\) is the bulk density of first fluid phase, \(\rho_2\) is the bulk density of second fluid phase, and \(\rho_s\) is the solid density. The total viscosity \(\eta\) is interpolated only with bulk properties of fluid phases:

(130)\[\frac{1}{\eta}=\frac{\phi}{\eta_1}+\frac{(1-\phi)}{\eta_2}\]

The harmonic mean is used.

List of input parameters in .ini file

Section [init_solid]

In .ini file, this section is necessary for initialization of funtion \(\psi\). Option solid_phase must be set equal to 1 to activate the solid phase:

  • solid_phase=1

Three other parameters are required in the model:

  • W_sol=: interface width of solid phase

  • rho_sol=: solid density

  • Mphi_sol=: interface mobility of solid phase

Warning

Even though it is possible to set different values, it is highly recommended to set identical values for solid phase and fluid phase interface properties: \(W_{\psi}=W_{\phi}=W_{\varphi}\) and \(M_{\psi}=M_{\phi}=M_{\varphi}\). In practice, the same parameter value must be set for W and W_sol and Mphi and Mphi_sol.

Next several parameters dependent of the initialization must set:

  • init_solid_type=: keyword of solid initial condition

If init_solid_type=container_hole, then the following parameters are required

  • x0_gl=

  • y0_gl=

  • width_gl=

  • height_gl=

  • thickness_gl=

  • h_glass=

  • length_hole_gl=

Other examples of initial conditions for \(\psi\) can be found inside folder run_training_lbm.

Section [move_solid]

In .ini file, this section is necessary for indicating how the solid phase moves. More particularly the parameters of this section are necessary for \(\boldsymbol{u}_s\) in Eq. (124).

  • move_solid_type=: keyword to indicate what sort of displacement is imposed.

Section [contact_angle]

In .ini file, this section is necessary for calculating the three surface tension forces. By default, only the capillary forcce between both fluid phases is computed. If contact_angle=1, then the two last forces of Eq. (127) are also computed.

  • contact_angle=1

If that option is equal to 1, then the values of surface tension between fluid 1 and solid \(\sigma_{1s}\) and between fluid 2 and solid \(\sigma_{2s}\) have to be set:

  • sigma_phi1_solid=: positive real value

  • sigma_phi2_solid=: positive real value

Examples of simulations with that model

Several examples are available in the folder run_training_lbm to practice two-phase flows interacting with a solid phase: see Run “Two-phase fluid flows interacting with a solid phase”.
